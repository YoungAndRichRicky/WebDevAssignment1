Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery


The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a sanctuary for endangered sea turtles. The hatchery, nestled in Sri Lanka's picturesque coastline, plays an important role in conserving these magnificent species and raising awareness about their situation. They committed to protecting the fragile marine habitat and preserving the survival of sea turtles for future generations at Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery.

Turtles Species in Kosgoda

Several intriguing turtle species may be found at the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, where they are cared for and protected by the Turtle Hatchery's committed personnel. Our hatchery houses the Green Turtle, Olive Ridley Turtle, Loggerhead Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, and Leatherback Turtle. These species, each with its own characteristics and conservation needs, find sanctuary in the capable hands of the dedicated crew. The opportunity to learn about these remarkable creatures, their life cycles, and the conservation activities made to preserve their well-being will be provided by visiting the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery.

Conservation Efforts

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is committed to a variety of conservation projects to safeguard sea turtles and their vulnerable habitats. They have undertaken the following significant initiatives:

Nesting Site Management
Along the Sri Lankan coastline, the crews actively identify and safeguard sea turtle breeding locations. In addition, the team monitors nesting activity, protects nests from predators, and provides a safe environment for nesting mothers and their eggs.


Turtle Rescue and Rehabilitation
The team rescues and heals wounded or sick sea turtles brought to the hatchery. Their knowledgeable staff provides medical treatment, nursing care, and rehabilitation programs to assist these turtles in recovering before returning them to the ocean.


Community Engagement
They work with local communities to actively engage them in sea turtle protection. This involves holding workshops, training programs, and livelihood projects to encourage sustainable behaviors and limit human effect on sea turtle numbers and ecosystems.


Research and Monitoring
To compile information on sea turtle populations, nesting habits, and migration routes, they carry out scientific studies and monitoring programs. This data supports broader sea turtle research initiatives and informs conservation measures.


Hatchery Operations
They provide the best conditions for the development of turtle eggs by maintaining a controlled environment within our hatchery. To increase the turtles' chances of surviving, they gently release the hatchlings into the ocean.

The Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery aims to have a positive effect on sea turtle populations and their habitats through these coordinated activities. They are convinced that they can guarantee a better future for these amazing species by spreading knowledge, carrying out research, and actively participating in local communities.
